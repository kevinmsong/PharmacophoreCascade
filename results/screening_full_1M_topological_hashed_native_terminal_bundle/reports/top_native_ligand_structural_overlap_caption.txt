Panels A and B show zoomed projected views of the aligned ligand against the local GLP-1 peptide segment surrounding the matched residues. Faint colored spheres show nearby retained native GLP-1 pharmacophore features, larger filled spheres show the matched native features, open spheres show the corresponding matched ligand-feature centroids, small colored atoms indicate the peptide atoms used to define each matched feature centroid, and dashed segments show the final feature correspondences used in the native scoring model.

Best-state descriptor interpretation: ZINCk700001bQgxo is a balanced-polar, balanced lipophilicity, formally 0-charged scaffold (MW 300.4, TPSA 107.4, logP 0.11) with an acceptor/anionic-biased pharmacophore profile. It reproduces 6 native GLP-1 interface features (30.0% weighted native coverage). It also reaches the native aromatic or hydrophobic anchor region. The ligand spans a broader feature distribution than most compact mimetics.
This figure should be interpreted as a pharmacophore-alignment result, not as an experimentally observed ligand-receptor complex. Its value is that it shows why the top ligand scored highly under the native branch: it captures a chemically coherent subset of the receptor-facing GLP-1 interaction pattern in the active `6X18` reference state.
