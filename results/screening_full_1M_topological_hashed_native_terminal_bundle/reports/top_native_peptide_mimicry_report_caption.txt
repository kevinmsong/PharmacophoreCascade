Figure caption
Residue-level analysis of how the highest-ranked native-scored small molecules reproduce the active GLP-1 peptide pharmacophore in the `full_1M_topological_hashed` screen. All panels use the best native-scored microstate/conformer per ligand from `ligand_best_native_mapping_summary.csv` and the retained 26-feature peptide reference defined in `native_reference_features.csv`.

Panel A summarizes the top 20 native-ranked ligands. Each heatmap cell is `100 * matched_residue_reference_weight / total_reference_feature_weight`, where `matched_residue_reference_weight` is the summed native-feature weight contributed by the matched feature(s) at that peptide residue for the ligand's best state, and `total_reference_feature_weight` is the sum of all 26 retained GLP-1 reference-feature weights (33.58). The aligned right-hand bar chart shows the total `native_weighted_coverage_pct` for the same ligands.
Panel B aggregates the top 100 native-ranked ligands into a residue-by-residue co-occurrence matrix. Each cell reports the percentage of ligands whose best state matches both residues at least once; diagonal cells therefore equal single-residue prevalence in the top-100 cohort.
Panel C reports single-residue prevalence across the same top-100 cohort, with bar colors indicating the native feature family. The overlaid black line is each residue's intrinsic share of the native model, computed as `100 * residue_weight / total_reference_feature_weight` after summing all retained features assigned to that residue.
Panel D compares cohort concentration to native-model expectation using an enrichment factor: `cohort_matched_share / native_model_share`, where `cohort_matched_share` is the residue's share of all matched reference weight accumulated across the top cohort and `native_model_share` is the residue's share of the original native reference model.

The dominant recapitulated peptide motif is a polar/anionic core centered on SER14 polar 99%; ASP15 anion 98%; SER18 polar 95%; SER17 polar 83%; GLU21 anion 50%. The strongest pairwise co-occurrence is `SER14` with `ASP15` at 97% of the top-100 cohort, indicating that these residues are usually recovered together rather than as isolated contacts. The highest enrichment factors are SER14 3.57x; ASP15 3.53x; SER18 3.43x, showing which peptide residues capture a disproportionately large share of cohort-level mimicry relative to their native model weight.
Taken together, the figure indicates that the top native-ranked ligands do not mimic the entire peptide uniformly. Instead, they repeatedly compress the receptor-facing GLP-1 interaction pattern into a smaller reusable motif dominated by the SER14/ASP15/SER17/SER18 core, with secondary variation contributed by residues such as GLU21, THR11, VAL16, ALA8, THR13, and GLU9.
